Chapter 1 — Key Concepts

Hover any term to learn it

Every technical word below has a plain-English tooltip. The same tooltips appear when you hover neurons, connections, and controls in the lab.

🧱
Layers
A neural network is a stack of layers. Each layer transforms the signal. More layers = deeper network = more abstract understanding. Deep learning just means many layers.
🎚️
Weights & Biases
Weights are the volume knobs on every connection — they decide how much one neuron influences the next. Biases are like the default volume when nothing comes in.
output = weight × input + bias
📉
Loss & Backprop
Loss measures how wrong we are. Backpropagation is the math trick that figures out which weights caused the error. Gradient Descent then nudges them to do better.
⚡
Activation Functions
Activation functions add non-linearity — without them, 10 layers = 1 layer mathematically. ReLU, Sigmoid, Tanh each behave differently.
🔁
Epochs & Batches
One epoch = one full pass through ALL your training data. The model sees every example, calculates its total error, then updates. Repeat hundreds of times.
🏗️
Architecture Effects
More neurons → more capacity but can overfit. More layers → learns hierarchical patterns. Too many → vanishing gradients.
